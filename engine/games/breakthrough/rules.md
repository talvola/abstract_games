# Breakthrough

A two-player race game on an **8×8** board (Dan Troyka, 2000). Player 0 is
**White** (pawns start on rows 1–2 and advance upward); player 1 is **Black**
(rows 7–8, advancing downward). Every pawn is identical.

## Moving

On your turn, move one pawn one square **forward**:

- **straight ahead** — only onto an **empty** square;
- **diagonally forward** — onto an empty square, **or** to **capture** an enemy
  pawn standing there.

You may **never** move sideways or backward, and you may **not** capture straight
ahead. There is no double-step and no promotion.

## Winning

You win the instant one of your pawns reaches the **opponent's home row** (row 8
for White, row 1 for Black). A player who has **no legal move** loses.

## Notes

- Because every move pushes a pawn one row forward (and captures also remove a
  pawn), play always makes progress — Breakthrough cannot cycle and has **no
  draws**.

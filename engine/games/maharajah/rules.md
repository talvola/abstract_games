# Maharajah and the Sepoys

A classic **asymmetric** chess variant: one lone super-piece against a full army.

## Board & sides

Standard 8x8 board.

- **Seat 0 — the Maharajah (White).** A *single* piece, the **Maharajah** (also
  called the Amazon or Rajah), starting on **e1**. It is this side's only piece
  and it is **royal**.
- **Seat 1 — the Sepoys (Black).** A *full* standard chess army — eight pawns and
  `R N B Q K B N R` — set up normally on the 7th and 8th ranks. The Sepoy **king**
  is royal in the usual way.

## How the pieces move

- The **Maharajah** moves as a **Queen *or* a Knight combined** (the fairy piece
  known as an *Amazon*): any number of empty squares orthogonally or diagonally,
  **or** a knight's L-jump. It captures normally.
- The **Sepoys** move by **normal chess rules** — with one exception below.

## The two special rules

1. **No Sepoy promotion.** A Sepoy pawn that reaches the last rank simply **stays
   a pawn**. (Promotion is forbidden; an unrestricted promoting army would make
   the lone Maharajah hopeless. This restriction is what makes the game a contest
   at all.)
2. **Both sides have a royal piece.** The Maharajah is royal for its side, exactly
   as the king is royal for the Sepoys. Neither royal may move into capture, and a
   side that cannot save its royal from capture has been *checkmated*.

## Turn order & winning

- **The Sepoys (Black) move first.**
- **White (the Maharajah) wins** by checkmating the **Sepoy king**.
- **Black (the Sepoys) wins** by checkmating — trapping and capturing — the
  **Maharajah**.
- There is no castling and no en-passant-relevant pawn for the Maharajah side
  (it has no pawns); the Sepoys' en passant is unaffected by these rules but never
  arises against a single enemy piece in practice.

## Draws / termination

To guarantee the game ends, the standard chess draw rules apply: **stalemate**
(the side to move has a royal but no legal move → draw), the **fifty-move rule**,
**threefold repetition**, and a hard **400-ply cap**. Insufficient-material draws
do not occur here (the Maharajah alone can always deliver mate, and the Sepoy army
is heavy material).

## Note

With perfect play the **Sepoys win** on the 8x8 board — the variant is *solved* in
the Sepoys' favour. It is enjoyed as a handicap / teaching game.

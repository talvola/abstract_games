# Legan Chess

A corner-to-corner chess variant invented by **L. Legan (1913)**. The whole game
is rotated to a diagonal orientation: **White attacks toward the a8 corner**
(up-left) and **Black toward the h1 corner** (down-right). Each army is arrayed
along the two board edges adjacent to its **own** corner, behind a diagonal
pawn wall.

## Objective
Checkmate the opponent's king — exactly as in standard chess.

## Starting position
On an ordinary 8x8 board:

- **White** — King h1, Queen g2, Bishops f1 & h2, Knights g1 & h3, Rooks e1 & h4;
  Pawns on d1, e2, e4, f2, f3, g3, g4, h5.
- **Black** — King a8, Queen b7, Bishops a7 & c8, Knights a6 & b8, Rooks a5 & d8;
  Pawns on a4, b5, b6, c6, c7, d5, d7, e8.

Black's position is the 180-degree rotation of White's. White moves first.

## The Legan pawn
A pawn advances toward the **enemy corner**:

- A **White** pawn **moves** one square diagonally up-left (toward a8) and
  **captures** one square orthogonally in that direction — i.e. one square left
  **or** one square up. Example: a White pawn on f3 may step to **e4**, and may
  capture on **e3** or **f4**.
- A **Black** pawn **moves** one square diagonally down-right (toward h1) and
  **captures** one square right **or** one square down.

There is **no two-square first move** and **no en passant** (the pawns do not
start on a home rank).

## Promotion
A pawn promotes (to Q, R, B or N — your choice) on reaching the **enemy corner**,
meaning either of the two far edges by that corner:

- **White** promotes on the **a-file** or the **8th rank**.
- **Black** promotes on the **h-file** or the **1st rank**.

## Everything else
Knights, bishops, rooks, queen and king move exactly as in standard chess.
**There is no castling.** Check, checkmate, stalemate and the draw rules
(fifty-move, threefold repetition, insufficient material) are all standard.

# Berolina Chess

Standard chess with one twist: the **Berolina pawn** (Edmund Hebermann, Berlin 1926).

## Objective
Checkmate the opponent's king — exactly as in standard chess.

## The Berolina pawn
The pawn's move and capture are **reversed** from an ordinary pawn:
- It **moves** one square **diagonally** forward (and may move two squares diagonally on its first move), onto empty squares only.
- It **captures** one square **straight** ahead.
- Therefore it **attacks (and gives check) straight ahead**, not on the diagonals.
- **En passant** applies to the diagonal double-step: the pawn is captured on the square it skipped over.

## Everything else
Knights, bishops, rooks, queen, king, **castling**, check/checkmate/stalemate, and all the draw rules (fifty-move, threefold repetition, insufficient material) are exactly as in standard chess. White moves first.

## Notes
Pawns are more mobile but defend less, so draws are rare.

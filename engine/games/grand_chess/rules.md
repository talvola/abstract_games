# Grand Chess

Christian Freeling's 10×10 chess (1984), with two extra pieces.

## Objective
Checkmate the opponent's king.

## Board & setup (10×10)
- Rooks in the corners of rank 1 / rank 10.
- On rank 2 / rank 9: **N B Q K M C B N**.
- Pawns on the **third** rank.

## The new pieces
- **Marshall (M)** — moves as a **rook + knight**.
- **Cardinal (C)** — moves as a **bishop + knight**.

## Play
All other pieces move as in standard chess. **There is no castling.** Pawns step one or two squares on their first move and capture diagonally; **en passant** applies.

### Promotion (the unusual rule)
A pawn may promote **only to a piece type its owner has lost** — so you can never field more of a piece than you started with. Promotion is **optional** on the 8th and 9th ranks and **compulsory** on the 10th. If no captured piece is available, a pawn cannot advance onto the final rank and is stuck on the 9th.

## Winning & draws
Checkmate wins; stalemate, the fifty-move rule, threefold repetition, and insufficient material draw.
